West Bengal's cool Himalayan foothills are the joy of the state. Snowy mountain views, emerald-green tea estates, colourful Tibetan monasteries and charming colonial-era architecture can be found all across the foothills. While treks and mountain biking draw the active, you could just as easily spend a couple of days learning about Darjeeling tea while staying on a tea plantation. And when you are finished, Sikkim is just a stone's throw away!


Dooars

The Dooars or Duars are the foothills of the eastern Himalayas in North-East India around Bhutan. Duar means 'door' and the region forms the gateway to Bhutan from India.

Gorumara

Gorumara National Park is an important national park of North Bengal. It is situated just at the foothills of Eastern Himalaya in Terai region, on the flood plains in Murti, Raidak.

Sundarbans

Apart from being a unique largest mangrove eco-system of the world, the Sundarbans has the world's largest deltaic mangrove forests and is also home to one of India's most iconic wildlife species - the Royal Bengal Tiger.
